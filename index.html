My Story

I was born in 2001, and my journey began a little differently from most. Soon after I was born, my family moved to the UK, where we lived in Brighton until 2010. I went to school there, travelled across parts of Europe with my parents, and grew up quietly observing the world around me.

I don’t remember having many close friends during those early years, but I remember curiosity. New places, unfamiliar faces, and long journeys shaped how I see the world even today. Travel still feels like home to me.

Roots & grounding

In 2010, we moved back to India and settled in Pune. I spent my school years there, built friendships that still matter deeply to me, and grew up in a humble, upper-middle-class household. That phase grounded me.

Even though life today looks very different, the middle-class mindset stayed with me — valuing stability, thinking rationally, and never taking comfort for granted.

Responsibility & becoming independent

I was a shy and introvert child, but responsibility found me early. I understood that one day I would need to carry my family forward — a quiet weight many middle-class Indian kids grow up with.

That realization shaped my drive. I worked with a simple goal in mind: to build a life where my family would never have to hesitate before choosing what they needed — or wanted.

Belief, patience, and perspective

Sports has quietly shaped how I think about life. Watching Lionel Messi persevere through years of heartbreak before finally winning the World Cup taught me patience, humility, and trust in the process.

I’ve learned that progress isn’t always visible, that you don’t win every day, and that showing up consistently matters more than timing.

Life hasn’t been perfectly linear. There were moments of self-doubt, loneliness, and feeling behind others. There were also moments that reminded me how fragile life can be — and how important gratitude truly is.

Today, I celebrate achievements, but I don’t let them define me. I try to stay grounded, open to growth, and grateful for everything that has brought me here.

More than anything, I hope to leave the world — however slightly — better than I found it. Even if something I do helps just one person, that would be enough.
